Reference — Every Sunday

Service Day Checklist

Complete every item in order before the congregation arrives. If any item cannot be completed, escalate to the tech director immediately — not at the service transition where it becomes visible.

Progress
0 / 0
Phase 1 Power Up & Board Load
Power on stage equipment and S32 stage box
Stage power first. Confirm S32 power LED is solid — no blinking. Blinking indicates no AES50 link yet.
Power on the X32
Allow 15 seconds for full boot. Do not interrupt power during boot sequence.
Load your baseline scene
Scenes menu → navigate to your baseline scene → LOAD → confirm. Verify channel names on scribble strips match the routing reference.
Critical
Confirm AES50 link — S32 connected via CAT6
ROUTING tab → AES50 A — confirm signal present on all S32 channels. If no link, check CAT6 cable at both ends.
Critical
Power on amplifiers and powered speakers — last
Amps always go on last. Confirm main LR fader is down before powering amps. Bring it up slowly after amps are on.
Critical
Phase 2 Line Check
Enable phantom power on all condenser and active DI channels
Verify 48V is active on required channels before setting gain. Check your channel routing reference for which channels require phantom.
Run line check — all drum channels in order
Kick, snare, toms, hi-hat, overheads. Set gain at performance level — not a tap, a real hit. Target –18 dBFS average with fader at unity.
Run line check — bass, guitars, keys, pads
Each instrument plays at performance level. Confirm signal on meter, set gain, move to next.
Run line check — all vocal channels
Each vocalist sings at full performance level — not a test whisper. The gain you set for quiet will clip when they open up during worship.
Critical
Confirm all wireless receiver channels — signal present
Check VM1, VM2, VM3, SPKR1, SPKR2, Sermon Headset. Wireless must show active RF signal at receiver before the service.
Confirm Spotify / playback source routing and level
AUX IN active, stereo pair linked, playback bus at reference pre-service level. Confirm no bleed into live channels.
Phase 3 IEM Setup
Confirm all IEM beltpacks are charged and powered on
4 Shure, 2 Sennheiser. Dead batteries are the most preventable IEM failure. Check before performers arrive.
Critical
Build IEM mixes with performers on stage
Use Sends on Fader for each bus. Each performer confirms their mix before leaving the stage. Do not skip this step.
Critical
Each performer verbally confirms IEM mix is good
Thumbs up from the stage is not enough. Confirm verbally or via talkback that each performer is satisfied.
Phase 4 Recording & Livestream
Arm Reaper — confirm signal on all tracks
Mackie Onyx interface connected, Reaper open, all tracks armed and showing input signal. Press record before the service starts.
Confirm Restream is active and showing signal
Livestream bus output level is appropriate. Restream dashboard shows incoming signal. The stream audience is real — treat this as a second congregation.
Coordinate with video operator — confirm ready status
Both operators confirm go status before the first song. Any unresolved issues are raised now, not during a transition.
Phase 5 Pre-Service & Final Checks
Start Spotify pre-service music — confirm level
Background level — warm, not a concert. All live channels confirmed muted. No bleed from open mics into the house.
Walk the room — confirm house mix sounds right from multiple positions
What you hear at FOH is not what the congregation hears. Walk to the back and sides before they arrive.
All unresolved issues escalated before congregation arrives
If something is not right, say so now. The tech director needs time to help. Silence until the first song is not acceptable.
